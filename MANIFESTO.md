# Manifesto

**One sentence.** A working-out-loud digital garden for how interfaces communicate — tokens, components, the small decisions — written so I can think, not to perform.

## Four pillars

1. **Tech & Craft.** Design systems, components, the grammar hiding in our tokens. How to make software that survives contact with real work — long essays on the model, short notes on the mess.
2. **Living Well.** Practices for the working life — focus, sleep, money, attention. What I tried and what stuck. Field reports, not advice.
3. **Meaning.** Essays on what the work is for, and how to keep asking. The why behind the how.
4. **Field Notes.** Short, imperfect observations. Seeds. The things I'm still chewing on. They earn the right to be called essays later, or they don't — both outcomes are fine.

## Voice

First person. Honest. No guru tone. "Here's what I tried" rather than advice. Imperfect thinking is allowed to ship, because perfect thinking never arrives.

## Maturity

Notes carry a growth marker — seedling 🌱, growing 🌿, evergreen 🌳. The garden is tended, not published. Things published as seedlings may stay seedlings forever, and that's fine; the gardener's job is to water what wants to grow, not to force blooms.

## What success looks like

In year two the site should look different from year one — grown, not redesigned. The trend lines on the pillars should be visible: more essays in the pillars I'm most uncertain about, fewer where I've said what I needed to say.

This manifesto is the thing every other piece of the site is tested against. If a choice doesn't serve one of the four pillars or the voice rule, it's the wrong choice.
