Ninety minutes, Sunday around six, before the new week starts to bleed into the
old one. The shape is simple: scan, sync, queue. That's it.

**Scan.** What actually happened this week, not what I remember wanting to
happen. I open the calendar, the bank, and a fresh notebook page. I write down
the three things that took more energy than I expected and the one thing that
took less. That asymmetry is usually the most useful signal in the week.

**Sync.** Move anything that drifted. A bill that came in late. A meeting that
should have been an email. A subscription I forgot I was paying for — there is
always at least one. The financial review is not about budgeting, it's about
noticing. Most weeks there is nothing to fix; the value is the audit.

**Queue.** Three things for Monday morning, written down before I close the
notebook. Not a full plan — just three things I want to walk in knowing I will
do. The rest of the week will happen to me, but Monday I get to choose.

This has been the most durable practice I have. It started in 2022 as a
half-hour scramble on Sunday night, and it has slowly earned its ninety
minutes. Skipping it for two weeks in a row is the canary — when I skip, the
following month is harder than it needs to be.
