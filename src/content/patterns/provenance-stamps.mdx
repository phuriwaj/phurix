A small piece of metadata does the work of an entire audit trail. *This is two years old. Reviewed by three engineers. Last edited on a Tuesday.* Single-line markers that say how recent, how reviewed, how trustworthy. Readers do not need provenance trails; they need provenance stamps.

## When to use it

- Documentation pages, design system catalogues, knowledge bases.
- Anywhere users cite the product as authority.

## When to avoid it

- Live, transient content (chat, presence, ephemeral state) where age is the message.

## Why a stamp, not a trail

A trail is for the people who need to defend a decision. A stamp is for the people who need to *trust* one. Most readers do not need the diff history of a paragraph — they need to know whether the paragraph is still considered correct. A stamp answers that question in a glance. A trail answers it in an hour.
